Homeowner Goals

Find out how much you need to save each month and the salary you need to buy a starter home based on your current age and where you live.

Either put the age you want to buy a home buy, or the amount you can put away each month. You cannot put both.

The maximum difference between your current age and the age you want to buy can be 33 years.

Methodology

1. Estimating Starter Home Prices

To estimate starter home prices in each area, we began by obtaining the median home prices from Zillow for the respective locations. Recognizing that starter homes are typically more affordable than the median-priced homes, we adjusted these figures by multiplying the median prices by two-thirds (⅔). This adjustment aims to reflect the cost of entry-level homes more accurately.

2. Projecting Future Home Prices (2026–2058)

We analyzed historical housing price data from 1991 to 2024 provided by the Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA). Using this data, we calculated the average annual growth rate of home prices over the period. We then applied this growth rate to project future starter home prices from 2026 through 2058, assuming a consistent rate of appreciation.

4. Calculating Property Taxes

Property taxes are dependend on your state and are taken from Business Insider's 2024 report.

3. Estimating Your Interest Rate

Your estimated mortgage interest rate is based on two key factors:

  • Your credit score
  • Your down payment size (as a percentage of the home's price)

We use a base interest rate depending on your credit score, following typical lender expectations according to The Mortgate Reports:

Credit ScoreBase Annual Interest Rate
Excellent (740+)6.5%
Good (700–739)6.75%
Fair (660–699)7.25%
Poor (620–659)8.0%
Very Poor (<620)9.5%

We then apply an adjustment based on your loan-to-value ratio (how much of the home’s price you're borrowing). A larger down payment means a lower risk for lenders and therefore a lower interest rate.

The following adjustments are applied to your base interest rate:

  • 20% or more down: -0.25%
  • 10%–19.99% down: 0.00%
  • 5%–9.99% down: +0.25%
  • Below 5% down: +0.50%

This weighting was based on Fannie May's Loan-Level Price Adjustment Matrix

Your final interest rate is calculated by adding the base rate from your credit score and the adjustment from your down payment.

5. Estimating Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is typically required for conventional loans when the down payment is less than 20% of the home's purchase price. The cost of PMI varies based on several factors, including the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio and the borrower's credit score. According to data from NerdWallet, PMI rates generally range from 0.58% to 1.86% of the original loan amount annually.

So we calculate PMI in a linear fashion with a 3% downpayment equating to 1.86% annual rate, and a 19% down payment equating to 0.58% annual rate.

These rates were applied to the loan amount to determine the annual PMI cost, which was then divided by 12 to obtain the monthly PMI payment.

6. Mortgage Payment Calculations

Monthly mortgage payments were calculated using the standard amortization formula, which accounts for the loan amount, interest rate, and loan term. The formula used is:

M = P × [r(1 + r)n] / [(1 + r)n − 1]

Where:
M = monthly mortgage payment
P = principal loan amount
r = monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
n = total number of payments (loan term in months)

The total estimated monthly payment includes:

  • Principal and interest payments
  • Monthly property tax (calculated as 1.235% of home value divided by 12)
  • Monthly homeowner's insurance (not detailed here but typically estimated)
  • Monthly PMI payment (if applicable)

7. Determining Required Salary

To ensure affordability, we calculated the minimum gross annual salary required so that the total monthly housing payment does not exceed 28% of the borrower's income. This threshold aligns with common lending guidelines that suggest housing costs should not surpass 28% of gross monthly income.
